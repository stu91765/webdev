The After care health centre

Designed to provide advice and helpful methods to better the physical and mental state of patients requiring aftercare.

Objective/Goals

The objective and goal are to secure enough funding to provide aftercare for patients requiring physical and psychologic Rehabilition after an injury or traumatic experience. The website will provide various channels, methods and online sessions in: - Physiotherapy, Occupational therapy, Nutrional support, Rehabilitation and Pain management, with nationwide workshops designed for social Rehabilition during after care nationwide.

The programme is design to decrease the pressure on the NHS and provide, different curing and maintaining methods to patients requiring after care primarily, and those who wish to take advantage of their ongoing health, both mentally and physically secondarily. This programme will also provide education for those who suffer from PTSD eating disorders, depression, stress disorder, social and mental wellbeing and even venture into substance use disorders. Increase in subscription by 10% within 9 months Provide helpful aftercare advice and be recommended by 55% of visitors within 9 months (S M A R T )

Targeted Key audiences

The targets/ key audience of the software service will be for the older demograph with the hope of stimulating their intellectual thinking via links to websites, apps with brain training games and physical activities via zoom or other channels for all.

    (1) The modality in which the subject performs: brain stimuli and motor gain seem to be greater with intense, active, assistive, and repetitive movements than with nonassistive or passive movements.
    (2) The graduation of amount and typology of feedback (visual, auditory [43], haptic feedback) in relation to the degree of active subject movements, or to the degree of patient’s attention or active participation: there is still a lack of knowledge on the actual relation between sensory information and patient engagement and effort. This relation should be further investigated to implement the novel robotic systems for rehabilitation, also integrating concepts of the virtual reality.
    (3) The multilinearity of the exercises that seems to induce more motor cortex excitation.

Cancer patients

According to research the most efficient forms of rehabilitation in cancer patients after care are Exercise, eat a balanced diet, maintain a healthy weight, get good sleep, reduce stress, avoid tobacco and limit the amount of alcohol you drink.

heart disease patients

One person dies every 34 seconds in the United States from cardiovascular disease. About 697,000 people in the United States died from heart disease in 2020—thats 1 in every 5 deaths are heart disease related Heart disease cost the United States about $229 billion each year from 2017 to 2018.3 This includes the cost of health care services, medicines, and lost productivity due to death.

Heart disease is one of the UKs biggest concerns within the NHS both with cost and labour during aftercare. The software programme After care health centre aims to provide nutrient advise and both after care and aid in preventing the rate of heart disease in the UK.

Patients with Orthopaedic injuries and Traumatic injuries

Orthopaedic injury after care focuses the physical therapy to provide extra strength flexibility and ranges of motion on the injured area this will provided by using a selector and following both universal recommendation and what has been advised by the surgeon ensuring it falls under common practise and advise.

Providing advice for medication, tips on physiotherapy and links to healthcare professionals will not only benefit the patient's requiring aftercare but in turn reduce the funding provided by the taxpayer.

Patients with Mental health conditions.

After care health centre will provide advice for patients suffering from conditions such as depression, anxiety disorders, eating disorders post-traumatic stress disorders. the selected conditions are the most common mental health conditions within the Uk, recently the awareness of mental health has risen its suggested this is due to covid and the cost of living.
